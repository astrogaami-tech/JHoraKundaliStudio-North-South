# Astrogaami – Dual Chart - North and South Indian Charts

**A single‑file HTML tool for Vedic astrologers** – import data from **Jagannatha Hora (JHora)**, assign positions manually, and export high‑quality South/North Indian chart images ready for publication.

![Screenshot](docs/screenshot.png)

## 🚀 Try it now

[Live Demo on GitHub Pages](Dual_chart_-_South_-_north-v3i.html)

## ✨ Features

- **JHora import** – paste the “Basic Data” section – planets, degrees, signs, and birth details (name, date, time, place) are auto‑filled.
- **Manual assignment** – click a planet, then a sign; enter degrees with a simple editor.
- **Two chart styles** – South Indian (grid) and North Indian (kite) – rendered in crisp SVG.
- **Export PNG** – export **single** (South or North) or **dual** (side‑by‑side) images with:
  - Clean title & birth details
  - Degree position summary with nakshatra, pada, karakas, and classical flags (C, GY, MB)
- **Colour palettes** – eight professional themes (Indigo, Maroon, Forest, etc.).
- **Responsive** – works on desktops, tablets, and laptops.
- **Privacy first** – all processing happens locally in your browser; no data is sent to any server.

## 🧰 Requirements

- Any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari).
- (Optional) [Jagannatha Hora (JHora)](https://www.vedicastrologer.org/jh/) to generate the “Basic Data” text for import.

## 📖 How to Use

1. **Open** `index.html` in your browser.
2. **Import from JHora** – click **⚡ JHora** and paste the “Basic Data” section from JHora (or manually assign planets via the sidebar).
3. **Tweak** – use the sidebar to change signs, edit degrees, or adjust birth details.
4. **Choose palette** – pick a colour theme that matches your publication.
5. **Export** – click **↓ Dual PNG** (both charts), **S·PNG** (South only) or **N·PNG** (North only).
6. The exported PNG includes a title, birth info, and the planetary position summary – ready for books, articles, or social media.

## 🖌️ Colour Palettes

The tool offers 8 professional palettes: Classic, Indigo, Maroon, Forest, Slate, Saffron, Purple, Teal. Each provides a single‑hue look with high contrast, perfect for print.

## 🛠️ Development
